Online Socialization

There are many ways to socialize online. While you might think that online socialization lacks many elements of real life socialization, a recent study by The MacArthur Foundation finds that there are many valuable things to be gained from the former, including: enhancement of friendships, extension of interests, and motivated self-learning. Below are some of my favorite ways to socialize online:

  1. Instant Messaging (IM) for friendly socialization


    		 The concept of IM is largely unchanged since its inception with ICQ and AOL 1.0 in the mid-90's. It provides users with the ability to communicate quickly with other users online. The main appeal for me (and probably for most others as well) is the capability to maintain fast open connections with many people at the same time. While phone calls generally only allow communication between two parties, most IM clients allow an unlimited number of simultaneous conversations.

    At present, there are many IM protocols (with their native clients), and at least as many multi-protocol clients. I presonally prefer native clients because they usually provide the best implementation of their own protocol. My favorite IM client is AOL Instant Messenger, simply because most of my friends use it. I have also been using Google Talk lately to accomodate those friends who prefer their chat and their email integrated.
